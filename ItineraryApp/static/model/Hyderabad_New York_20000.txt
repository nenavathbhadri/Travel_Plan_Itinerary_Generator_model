Given your budget of $20,000 for a trip from Hyderabad to New York, I've crafted a comprehensive 14-day itinerary that includes a mix of historical sightseeing, cultural experiences, and delicious food. This plan will utilize various travel websites, hotel booking platforms, tourist guides, transportation schedules, and weather forecasts to ensure a memorable journey.

### Day 1-2: Travel from Hyderabad to New York

- **Flights:** Book your flights from Hyderabad (HYD) to New York (JFK) using websites like Expedia, Kayak, or directly through airlines like Emirates, Qatar Airways, or British Airways. With your budget, you can opt for a business class ticket, which should cost around $8,000-$10,000 for a round trip, depending on the airline and time of booking.
- **Accommodation:** For the first two nights, book a hotel in Manhattan. Use booking platforms like Booking.com, Hotels.com, or Marriott.com. The Plaza Hotel or similar luxury hotels will offer you a great experience. Cost for two nights could be around $1,500-$2,000.

### Day 3-4: Exploring New York

- **Sightseeing:** 
  - Day 3: Visit the Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island. Use the official Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island website for tickets ($21.50 per person).
  - Day 4: Explore the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Tickets can be booked on their official website ($25 per person).
- **Cultural Experience:** Spend an evening at Broadway. Book tickets for a musical on Telecharge or Ticketmaster. Prices vary depending on the show, but budget around $100-$150 per ticket.
- **Food:** Try some of the best pizza in New York at Lombardi's or Joe's Pizza. Also, visit the Ellenâ€™s Stardust Diner for a unique dining experience.
- **Transportation:** Use the NYC subway for most travels. Buy a MetroCard and refill as necessary. A 7-day unlimited ride pass costs $33.
- **Accommodation:** Continue your stay at The Plaza Hotel or similar.

### Day 5-6: Historical New York

- **Sightseeing:**
  - Day 5: Visit the 9/11 Memorial & Museum. Book tickets on their website ($24 per person).
  - Day 6: Explore Central Park and visit the Central Park Zoo. Entrance to the park is free, but zoo tickets can be booked on the Central Park Zoo website ($19.95 per person).
- **Cultural Experience:** Visit the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA). Tickets can be booked on their website ($25 per person).
- **Food:** Dine at Carbone for an upscale Italian-American experience. Budget around $100 per meal.
- **Accommodation:** Same as before.

### Day 7-8: Brooklyn and Beyond

- **Sightseeing:**
  - Day 7: Explore Brooklyn. Visit the Brooklyn Bridge, Brooklyn Museum (suggested admission $16 per person), and walk around DUMBO.
  - Day 8: Take a day trip to the Hudson Valley. Visit the Vanderbilt Mansion National Historic Site (free admission, but guided tours may have a fee).
- **Cultural Experience:** Attend a music or comedy show at the Brooklyn Academy of Music (BAM). Ticket prices vary.
- **Food:** Try some of the best bagels in New York at Russ & Daughters Cafe on the Lower East Side.
- **Transportation:** For the Hudson Valley trip, rent a car for the day (around $100) or use public transportation (around $20-$30 per person, round trip).
- **Accommodation:** Return to Manhattan for the night.

### Day 9-14: More of New York and Departure

- **Sightseeing:**
  - Spend the next few days exploring any sights you might have missed, revisiting favorites, or taking day trips to nearby cities like Philadelphia or Boston.
- **Cultural Experiences:** Continue to immerse yourself in the city's cultural offerings, from museums to performances.
- **Food:** Continue to explore the diverse culinary scene of New York.
- **Departure:** On your last day, ensure you are at JFK Airport with plenty of time to spare for your return flight.
